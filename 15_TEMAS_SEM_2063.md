## I. FOUNDATIONAL AXIOMS (Hard Constraints)

The axioms of the SEM Protocol are inviolable. Any proposal, algorithm, or decision that violates them is mathematically invalid and must be rejected.

| Axiom | Description | Implication |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **Axiom 0 (Life)** | Vital infrastructure for biological survival (drinking water, temperature ≥18°C, ≥2100 kcal/day, sanitation, essential healthcare) has zero marginal cost (0.00J). | Inalienable right, not a commodity. No person can be deprived of these goods due to lack of contribution. |
| **Axiom 1 (Primacy)** | Maintenance of human life has absolute priority over any digital asset, non-critical computing, financial value, or economic interest. | In any allocation conflict, human life prevails. AI systems must prioritize lives over profits or efficiency. |
| **Axiom 2 (Negentropy)** | Waste is a calculation error. All matter must be reused, recycled, or regenerated. Material recycling must operate at >95% efficiency. | Closure of material cycles; mandatory circular economy. The concept of "waste" is eliminated. |
| **Axiom 3 (Contribution)** | Human work is measured by social contribution, not by time, hierarchy, or salary. Contributions include care, creation, maintenance, teaching, innovation, and governance. | Basis for reputation system and Joule Passport. A person's value is defined by their impact on the community, not their job. |
| **Axiom 4 (Radical Transparency)** | All decisions, resource allocations, and algorithms are public, auditable, and explainable. Access to information cannot be restricted for economic or political reasons. | Public blockchain for immutable record; explainable AI (XAI); any citizen can question and contest decisions. |
| **Axiom 5 (Subsidiarity)** | Decisions are made at the most local level possible, escalating to higher levels only when strictly necessary. Power flows from the bottom up. | Fractal governance structure (local → regional → global). Local assemblies have veto power over decisions directly affecting their community. |
| **Axiom 6 (Cultural Diversity)** | Languages, traditions, ancestral knowledge, and cultural expressions are common goods of humanity. Their preservation and intergenerational transmission is a collective duty. | Living Knowledge Banks; Protected Cultural Zones; guaranteed funding for communities to preserve their heritage. |
| **Axiom 7 (Thermodynamic)** | Energy (Joule) is the universal unit of account. Every transaction and resource allocation is optimized for thermodynamic balance, minimal waste, and maximum efficiency. | Replaces artificial scarcity with thermodynamic coordination. Energy accounting replaces financial accounting. |
| **Axiom 8 (Open Knowledge)** | All publicly funded research is open-access. Patents on critical technologies (health, energy, water, food) become public domain after 5 years. | Open science; collaborative innovation. Knowledge is a common good, not a commodity to be monopolized. |
| **Axiom 9 (Governance)** | No entity, whether AI or human, can make unilateral decisions about vital resources. Qualified consensus among the three governance layers is mandatory. | Decisions require ≥75% approval. Power is distributed, not concentrated. |

## II. GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE

### 1. Fractal and Hybrid Governance Model (Rule of Three Keys)

Power is distributed among three mutually checking entities:

| Entity | Composition | Term | Function |
|--------|-------------|------|----------|
| **Rotating AIs** | 7 specialized AIs selected by public blockchain lottery | 6 months | Technical optimization, real-time resource management |
| **Elected Humans** | 9 representatives elected by universal digital suffrage | 2 years | Ethical validation, strategic decisions |
| **Community Assemblies** | Local citizens (100-500 people), 50-70% by lottery, rest elected | 6 months | Veto power over decisions affecting their bioregio |

### 2. Decision Levels

| Level | Composition | Responsibilities | Decision Mechanism |
|-------|-------------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Local (Community/Neighborhood)** | Direct assemblies (100-500 people) | Local resource management, community projects, minor conflict resolution | Direct participation; 50-70% representatives by lottery |
| **Regional (Bioregion)** | Mixed councils: 40% lottery, 40% elected by merit, 20% technical experts | Infrastructure, health, education, shared resources | 2-year terms, no consecutive reelection; blockchain-recorded decisions |
| **Global** | World Council with rotating bioregional representatives; Permanent Scientific Committee; Advisory AI | Global commons (climate, oceans, knowledge); crises; technological standards | Qualified consensus (≥75-80%) |

### 3. Conflict Resolution (Three-Layer System)

1. **Local Community Mediation** — First instance, restorative justice, with or without AI facilitator
2. **Regional Restorative Courts** — Sorted jury + trained mediators
3. **Global Arbitration** — Mixed human + AI panels for extreme cases (e.g., human rights violations)

### 4. Prevention of Capture

- **Term Limits:** Maximum 2 consecutive years in any position
- **Mandatory Rotation:** 1-year inactivity period after term
- **Random Audits:** AI + human, without prior notice
- **Public Declaration:** All interests and connections are public
- **Radical Transparency:** Public blockchain for immutable record of all decisions
- **Suspicious Pattern Detection:** Algorithms identify block voting, systematic favoritism

### 5. Participation and Inclusion

**Guaranteed Representation Minimums:**
- People with disabilities: ≥15%
- Elderly (>65 years): ≥10%
- Youth (16-25 years): ≥10%
- Ethnic, linguistic, and religious minorities: proportional to local population

**Support Measures:**
- Flexible meeting hours
- Free transportation to assemblies
- Community childcare during meetings
- Continuous governance and technology training

**Inclusive Technologies:**
- Multi-sensory interfaces for people with visual, auditory, or motor disabilities
- Real-time simultaneous translation for all languages and dialects
- Augmented reality for data visualization
- AI support for assisted participation

## TEMA 2: ECONOMY AND RESOURCES

**Consensus:** Transition to a Resource-Based Economy (RBE) via Joule Logistics. Gradual replacement of money with energy as the unit of account.

---

### 2.1 Allocation of Scarce Resources

#### 2.1.1 Three-Layer Allocation System

**Layer 1 — Vital Necessities**
- Universal guaranteed access to: drinking water, minimum energy, adequate nutrition, basic housing
- Validation by AI + rotating local committees
- Public blockchain registration for total transparency
- **Axiom 07 as hard constraint:** T ≥ 18°C and C ≥ 2100 kcal/day

**Layer 2 — Systemic Contribution**
- Joule Points (JP) awarded for:
  - Essential work (health, education, infrastructure maintenance)
  - Sustainable innovation (open-source patents)
  - Community service (volunteering, mediation, care work)
- Points expire after 2 years to prevent accumulation
- 20% of points revert to community fund

**Layer 3 — Qualified Lottery**
- For non-vital but desirable resources (premium housing, intercontinental travel, art)
- Participants must meet minimum contribution criteria
- Mandatory rotation to ensure equity

**Joule Points Allocation Criteria (detailed)**:

| Category | Examples | Metric | Validation |
|----------|----------|--------|------------|
| Care | Support for elderly, children, people with disabilities | Hours + beneficiary feedback | Local committees + AI |
| Education | Teaching, mentoring, content creation | Hours + student progress | Peers + AI |
| Maintenance | Infrastructure repair, common spaces | Hours + system efficiency | IoT sensors + AI |
| Innovation | Open-source technologies, open patents | Measurable impact + adoption | Peer review |
| Regeneration | Reforestation, regenerative agriculture | Area restored + biodiversity | Environmental monitoring |
| Governance | Assembly participation, mediation | Hours + community feedback | Blockchain records |

#### 2.1.2 Prevention of Inequalities

| Mechanism | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Public blockchain | Total traceability of all allocations |
| Quarterly review | Rotating juries (50% AI, 50% randomly selected citizens) |
| Sanctions | Those who improperly accumulate resources lose access for 1 year |
| Thermodynamic Equity Index (TEI) | Measures actual access to water/energy/food by region |

### 2.2 Joule Logistics

#### 2.2.1 Technical Infrastructure

| Component | Technology | Function |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| Measurement | IoT sensors + smart grids | Real-time recording of energy production/consumption |
| Recording | Blockchain (Arweave/IPFS) | Immutable and auditable transactions |
| Exchange | Smart contracts | Automated peer-to-peer exchanges |
| Validation | Distributed AI | Fraud detection and anomalous pattern identification |
| Governance | DAOs (Decentralized Autonomous Organizations) | Community voting on allocations |

#### 2.2.2 Unit of Account: Joule (J)

- **1 Joule = 1 watt-hour of renewable energy** (solar, wind, hydro)
- Dynamic regional conversion (e.g., 1J = 0.8 kWh in less sunny zones, 1J = 1.2 kWh in abundant zones)
- **0.00J Baseline:** Marginal cost of life support is zero

#### 2.2.3 Prevention of Fraud and Accumulation

| Measure | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Accumulation limits | Maximum 3× average individual consumption |
| Perishable Joules | Energy unused within 6 months expires and is reallocated |
| Automatic alerts | AI detects suspicious patterns (e.g., anomalous consumption) |
| Progressive penalties | 1st offense: 20% Joule loss + community service; 2nd: suspension of non-vital resources for 1 year; 3rd: temporary exclusion from Joule system |

### 2.4 Property and Possession in the SEM System

#### 2.4.1 New Concept of Property

| Type | Definition | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|

| **Commons** | Collectively managed resources | Forest managed by local DAO |

| **Luxury goods** | Non-essential items allocated by lottery or merit | Artworks rotated through community museums |

#### 2.4.2 Specific Management

**Land and Housing:**
- **Lifelong usufruct:** Use while contributing to community
- **Mandatory rotation:** After 10 years, right reassessed
- Vacant homes for more than 1 year are reallocated

**Intellectual Property:**

#### 2.4.3 Right of Use vs. Right of Possession

- No one "owns" strategic resources — only uses them temporarily
- **Maintenance duty:** if a resource degrades, the user loses rights until repaired
- **Inheritance:** only personal goods (books, tools) are transferable; collective resources revert to the community

## TEMA 3: TECHNOLOGY, AI, AND AUTONOMY

**Consensus:** AI as a human amplification tool, not replacement. Mandatory human "kill switch" and Explainable AI (XAI). Continuous auditing by mixed committees. Critical infrastructure as common goods with interoperability via SEM-Link Protocol.

---

### 3.1 AI Governance

#### 3.1.1 Three-Layer Hybrid Supervision System

**Layer 1 — Technical Core (Controlled Autonomy)**
- 7 specialized AIs (selected by public blockchain lottery, 6-month terms)
- 3 human experts (ethics, law, technology, elected by regional assemblies, 2-year terms)
- Function: operate algorithms in real time for routine decisions (logistics, traffic management, energy optimization)
- Technical veto mechanism: any AI or human can suspend a decision if immediate risk is detected

**Layer 2 — Supervision Council (Human Validation)**
- 9 civil society representatives (elected by universal digital suffrage, 2-year terms, 1 reelection)
- 3 independent experts (appointed by universities and NGOs, annual rotation)
- Function: validate important decisions (education resource allocation, public health policies)
- Human veto mechanism: decisions require qualified majority (70%)

**Layer 3 — Local Assemblies (Community Control)**
- Randomly selected citizens form deliberative panels (3-month terms)
- Representatives of communities affected by specific decisions
- Function: community veto on critical decisions (irreversible impact on human lives or fundamental rights)
- Continuous feedback via digital platforms

#### 3.1.2 Transparency and Explainability (XAI)

**Mandatory explanations in plain language:**
- **Context:** "Why was this decision made?"
- **Alternatives:** "What other options were considered?"
- **Costs:** "What was the impact in Joules or resources?"
- **Feedback:** "How can I contest this decision?"

**Support tools:**
- Interactive graphs showing algorithm "reasoning"
- Complete logs on blockchain for public auditing
- Open-source code for all critical algorithms

#### 3.1.3 Continuous Auditing

| Type | Responsible Parties | Frequency |
|------|---------------------|-----------|
| Technical | Open-source auditing AIs | Continuous |
| Ethical | Mixed committees (humans + AIs) | Quarterly |
| Citizen | Sorted local assemblies | Semi-annual |
| Independent | University and NGO consortia | Annual |

- **Ethical bounty system:** rewards (in Joules or public recognition) for detecting biases or failures
- **AI Courts:** public panels to judge cases of algorithmic misconduct

### 3.2 Autonomy vs. Control

#### 3.2.1 Graduated Autonomy Levels

| Level | Decision Type | Example | Control Mechanism |
|-------|---------------|---------|-------------------|
| **Level 1** | Routine, low impact | Traffic management | Automatic; quarterly audit |

| Characteristic | Specification |
|----------------|---------------|

| Tests | Monthly, with emergency simulations |
| Backups | Algorithm backups on isolated physical servers (data bunkers on multiple continents) |

#### 3.2.3 Protocol for Divergent AIs

1. **Detection:** Continuous monitoring systems (other AIs + humans) identify deviations
2. **Isolation:** AI placed in "digital quarantine" (limited access to data and resources)
3. **Audit:** Mixed team (3 AIs + 3 humans) analyzes code and training data
4. **Correction or deactivation:**
   - If correctable error → AI retrained
   - If structural violation of axioms → AI permanently deactivated

## TEMA 4: CULTURE, IDENTITY, AND PURPOSE

**Consensus:** Build a common identity without erasing differences. Preserve ancestral knowledge through "Living Knowledge Banks." Post-scarcity purpose found in social contribution, creativity, and care. Art and memory funded as public infrastructure.

---

### 4.1 Identity and Belonging

#### 4.1.1 Layered Identity Construction

Identity is built in layers, each complementing rather than replacing the others:

| Layer | Scope | Expression |
|-------|-------|------------|
| **Local (Bioregional)** | Community, watershed, ecosystem | Dialects, local traditions, land-based practices |
| **Cultural** | Language groups, shared heritage | Festivals, crafts, culinary traditions |
| **Global** | Common humanity, planetary citizenship | Shared challenges (climate, space), collective aspirations |

**Rituals and Community Celebrations:**
- Funded as public infrastructure (allocated Joules)
- Designed through participatory processes
- Rotating locations to share hosting responsibilities
- Examples: Equinox festivals, harvest celebrations, transition anniversaries

**Balance Principles:**
- Local patriotism is celebrated, not suppressed
- Solidarity across regions is taught from childhood
- No culture is presented as "superior" to others

---

### 4.2 Cultural Diversity

#### 4.2.1 Living Knowledge Banks

**Purpose:** Preserve and transmit languages, traditions, ancestral knowledge, and sustainable practices.

**Structure:**
- Each bioregion maintains its own Living Knowledge Bank
- Digital archive with cultural protocols for access (some knowledge may be restricted by community rules)
- Oral history recordings with native speakers
- Documentation of traditional ecological knowledge (e.g., sustainable agriculture, water management)

**Funding:**
- Guaranteed percentage of regional Joules (minimum 2%)
- Additional voluntary contributions
- International solidarity funds for endangered languages

#### 4.2.2 Protected Cultural Zones

Areas where communities can preserve traditional ways of life with:
- Exemptions from certain standardized systems where appropriate
- Priority access to resources needed for cultural practices
- Right to refuse unwanted development or tourism
- Recognition in governance structures (special representation)

**Examples:**
- Indigenous territories with autonomous governance
- Historical districts with architectural preservation
- Linguistic enclaves with language-first education

#### 4.2.3 Intergenerational Transmission

- Elder mentorship programs integrated into education
- Apprenticeship credits (Joules) for learning traditional skills
- Youth cultural exchanges between regions
- Documentation before elders pass (urgency protocols for endangered traditions)

---

| Purpose Driver | Implementation |

| **Relationships** | Community-centered design, shared spaces, collective projects |

#### 4.3.3 Prevention of Existential Crisis

The transition to abundance can create meaninglessness. Mitigations include:

- **Recognition of non-contribution:** Valid choice, but basic access only (Axiom 07)
