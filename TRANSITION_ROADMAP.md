# SEM 2063 — Transition Roadmap

## From 2026 to 2063: A Phased Civilizational Transition

This document defines the four phases of the Great Transition, with measurable entry and exit criteria for each. It is a living document — updated as phases are completed and evidence accumulates.

---

## Phase 0 — Foundation (2026–2030)

**Objective:** Build the technical, social and political foundations for the transition.

**Entry criteria:** Protocol v1.4 ratified and published. At least one human architect and one institutional partner committed.

**Key actions:**
- Complete technical specification of all protocol components
- Launch minimum 3 controlled pilots in volunteer communities (target: 1 in Europe, 1 in Latin America, 1 in Africa or Asia)
- Build cross-sector coalitions (local governments, universities, civil society organisations)
- Develop open-source tooling: Joule Passport prototype, Community Assembly governance kit, Living Infrastructure standards
- Establish the first Community Assemblies with full operational detail
- Document all pilot results publicly and adversarially — including failures

**Exit criteria:**
- At least 1 pilot demonstrates Axiom 07 compliance for 12 consecutive months
- At least 1 Community Assembly has exercised its veto power and the escalation process has been tested
- Protocol failure criteria (Axiom 00) have been triggered and resolved at least once in a controlled setting

## Phase 0.5 — Constitutional & Pluralistic Pilots (2026–2028)

**Objective**: Demonstrate that the enhanced SEM framework (SEM Constitution + updated Conflict Grammar with Active Pluralism) can function in real-world conditions and achieve broad agreement across diverse groups.

**Key Actions**:
- Launch 2–3 small-scale pilots (one urban DAO/community, one rural or indigenous-aligned group, one international online assembly).
- Implement full tracking of Joule flows + immutable ethical memory registry.
- Apply the complete Conflict Grammar process (including Reflective Dialogue, Steerable Perspectives, and Distributional Scoring) in at least one documented conflict.
- Conduct mandatory constitutional self-critique on all major decisions.
- Publish all outputs transparently (Git-style versioning + public dashboard).
- Measure success not only by adoption metrics but by participant satisfaction across majority and minority perspectives.

**Success Criteria**:
- At least 70% of participants (including minorities) report that their core values were respected.
- No Tier 1 violations recorded.
- At least one conflict successfully resolved using the new pluralistic mechanisms.
- Independent audit by external human observers confirms “promise, measure, correct” was followed.

This early pilot phase directly addresses the main reservation raised in the Rigorous Consensus of 20 AIs: the realism of transition.

## Phase 1 — Demonstration (2030–2040)

**Objective:** Prove the model works at city scale across different cultural and political contexts.

**Entry criteria:** Phase 0 exit criteria met. Minimum 3 documented pilots with 12-month track records.

**Key actions:**
- Expand to volunteer cities and regions (target population covered: 500,000+)
- Measure and publish impact: Axiom 07 compliance rates, Community Assembly participation, Joule Passport adoption
- Refine protocol based on evidence — including formal amendments via expanded quorum
- Develop anti-capture mechanisms: identify and document attempts to co-opt the protocol by financial or political actors
- Launch CONFLICT_GRAMMAR.md test cases in real community disputes
- Begin Living Infrastructure retrofits in public buildings in pilot cities

**Exit criteria:**
- At least 5 cities with 80%+ Axiom 07 compliance for 24 consecutive months
- Joule Passport adopted by 30%+ of population in at least 2 pilot regions
- At least 3 documented conflict resolutions using the Conflict Grammar framework
- No successful protocol capture documented

---

## Phase 2 — Scale (2040–2053)

**Objective:** Expand to mass adoption while protecting the protocol from capture and drift.

**Entry criteria:** Phase 1 exit criteria met. Anti-capture mechanisms tested and operational.

**Key actions:**
- Expand to all willing cities and regions globally
- Integrate Living Infrastructure standards into building codes in at least 10 jurisdictions
- Joule Passport interoperability across regions
- Strengthen Community Assembly network — cross-regional coordination
- Continuous adversarial validation: publish annual reports on protocol stress tests
- Manage coexistence period with legacy financial systems — document transition friction and solutions

**Exit criteria:**
- 50%+ of global population covered by Axiom 07 monitoring
- Living Infrastructure standards adopted in at least 20 jurisdictions
- Joule Passport recognised as valid identity for public services in at least 5 countries
- Zero instances of Axiom 07 violation sustained beyond 30 days in covered territories

---

## Phase 3 — Consolidation (2053–2063)

**Objective:** Complete integration as civilizational infrastructure. The protocol becomes self-sustaining.

**Entry criteria:** Phase 2 exit criteria met.

**Key actions:**
- Full global coverage of Axiom 07 monitoring
- Joule Passport replaces financial identity for all public services in participating territories
- Living Infrastructure standard for all new construction globally
- Legacy financial systems operate only in Autonomy Space domains
- Protocol governance fully decentralised — no single human or AI node holds disproportionate influence

**Exit criteria (2063 target state):**
- 90%+ of global population under Axiom 07 hard constraint
- 80%+ of new construction meets Living Infrastructure standards
- Community Assemblies operational in all covered territories
- Protocol has survived at least one Fundamental Review (Axiom 00) and emerged stronger

---

## Anti-Capture Principles

The transition is vulnerable to capture at every phase. The following principles apply throughout:

1. **Transparency by default:** All protocol decisions, amendments and audit results are public.
2. **No proprietary forks:** Any implementation of SEM must remain open-source and auditable.
3. **Rotating authority:** No AI node or human body holds permanent governance power.
4. **Adversarial documentation:** Capture attempts are documented publicly, not suppressed.
5. **Community veto is inviolable:** No phase advancement can override an active Assembly veto.
